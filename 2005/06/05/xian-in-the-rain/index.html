Xian in the rain

Filed under: China,Uncategorized — Amy @ 12:11 am

So, we get to Xian, get to the hotel by 6Y taxi, and then it starts raining. At first it’s just a little rain, the kind you can ignore. So we have free breakfast in the lounge, take much-needed showers and get ready to go get train tickets, do some shopping and some sightseeing.

By the time we step out the door of the hotel, it’s seriously raining. You know, the kind of rain that soaks your pants through in about 2 seconds, umbrella or no.

At this point, I ran back upstairs for my windbreaker/rain coat. Because, unlike Beijing, it’s actually cool here in Xian during the rain storms!

So, suitably attired, we cross the street for the railbooking office. Back in Beijing, the hotel concierge wrote out a note for us to make it easier to buy the train tickets. We showed this to the agent, she wrote down a price – 1299Y. Cash only.

Between us, we only had about 900.

So, back across the street to the hotel where I was able to exchange more traveller’s cheques. Unlike Russia and Mongolia, it’s actually fairly easy here in China.

And then back across the street again to actually buy the train tickets.

The past few trains, we’ve had two bottom bunks. This is nice in China because you get more room for your luggage under the side table, and because the top bunk folk typically only stay in the compartment when sleeping. Otherwise, there are chairs in the hallway. So it’s almost like having your own compartment.

Except, of course, for the girl’s father who kept coming into our room last night to get something out of his backpack, which was for some reason, in the storage space above the door. But I suspect Geoff will have more to say about that.

Anyhow, the two bottom bunk thing has been working well. However, the agent didn’t really understand the note (or it wasn’t written that way), so we have one lower and one upper bunk. Not the end of the world.

Here’s a shot of our last train, from Shanghai to Xian.

It’s a 27 hour train trip, so hopefully it all goes smoothly. And now that I’ve repacked my backpack only with souvenirs and have kept the rest of my stuff in the little suitcase, it shouldn’t be as hard to fit it all in the meager storage space available.

We also managed to book the full tour for tomorrow. 9.5 hours, it includes a trip out to the Terracotta warriors, the banpo village, the big goose pagoda, and several other stops. Should be a full day! I’m just hoping the bus is air conditioned and non-smoking.

If it stops pouring, it will be nice to check out the historical city walls of Xian, as well as the bell tower. However, so far there are no signs of it lightening up at all, much less any time soon.

The backup plan? Don’t ask me! We may just meander down this road a bit longer, maybe check out the hotel gym/pool/spa. For now, though, life is ok. The internet access at this cafe is cheap (4Y/hr), they have food and beverages, and the temperature is fine. I can’t seem to connect to my camera, but I don’t have many new photos since yesterday’s post.
