It should be interesting. At least we’re both in the same boat, eh buddy?

I’m cancelling my remaining two trainer appointments for next week. I’m so overwhelmed and my body seems to be throwing in the towel. The latest abandonment is my right knee, which I’ve never had a problem with before. Now it hurts. Every once in awhile, and thankfully, not when walking. But I don’t want to risk it. Ugh.

If I could manage to get a full nights’ sleep just ONCE this week I would be thrilled. Last night, Cleo had several really nasty coughing fits. I need to call the vet again today when I’m back from the gym. There’s got to be something better for her that won’t cause her poor little immune system to be even more compromised.

And, I have a tracking number for the train tickets. 8495 6295 0162 via FedEx. They’ll be here on Monday.

When I’m back from the gym I’m going to get reorganized and figure out exactly what’s left to do in the next week. Reasonably speaking, at least. Some things have got to be axed from the list. They just have to.

14 days and counting…

When we started planning this many months ago, there was a certain amount of disassociation. We weren’t really going on this trip, it was just an idea. Despite all the things that we’ve done, all the visas that we’ve applied for, it’s still felt like that to a large degree.

Until now.

Fourteen days. At this time, two weeks from now, I’ll be flying to Seattle.

I’ll probably be in shock, having left Alex behind. This is not something I’m looking forward to. Months ago, it didn’t seem such a huge deal. But with the wedding almost completely planned, I feel so much closer to Alex than I ever thought possible. Two weeks from today, I’ll probably be a wreck. I’ll be missing her within minutes, aching within hours.

And yet I’m still going.

I need to. This is something I’ve longed to do for almost half of my life (to this point, anyway). I want to dedicate myself to Alex as a partner, and not continue to long for an experience dreamt of for years. I’m excited and terrified beyond words. I feel like both running forward, and hiding under my bed.

Fourteen days. Getting there will will take forever, and be over in an instant.

Russian visa has arrived!

Phewf!

The one that worried me the most has finally arrived. Canada Post doesn’t exactly make it easy to track their packages, so it wasn’t easy to know where my poor passport was. Yes, there is a page for tracking packages, but they don’t update it frequently enough. It’s virtually useless for Priority Courier packages.

Oh, and “Priority Courier” is a bit of a laugh. If it hits the mail too late in the day, too bad, it’s not going to be there the next day. Gimme a freakin’ break! Why would I fork out so much to courier something quickly if I don’t want it somewhere quickly?!

Grr.

Anyway, onto the Mongolian passport. Prepare to have another $160 disappear from my poor bank account.
