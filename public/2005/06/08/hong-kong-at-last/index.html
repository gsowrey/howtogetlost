Hong Kong at Last

Filed under: China,Uncategorized — Amy @ 11:01 pm

After 27 hours on the overnight train from Xian, followed by a frenetic 2 hours in Guangzhou’s train stations (central and east), we hopped on the express through train and made it to Kowloon 2 hours later. Looking at the Lonely Planet map, it wasn’t immediately obvious how we would make it across the harbour to Hong Kong Island and the Cosmopolitan Hotel.

However, there were helpful signs that led us here, with a short little walk.

I had managed to wash my hair in the train sink that morning, but even so, it was good to be breathing fresh, if incredibly humid, air once again.

The harbour is even busier than you could imagine. Ships everywhere, loading containers, ferrying passengers, you name it!

I don’t think I’ve posted a photo of by “Barbie” suitcase.

When she’s sitting next to my army-green rugged Eagle Creek backpack, I call it the “butch-femme” luggage set. Nice, eh? I joke, but really, the pink and grey rolling case is pretty nice looking and decently made. It’s a bit of a pain to lug around, but it was less than half what shipping the souvenirs from Mongolia would have cost. So I’m not really complaining. I also have things organized now so that all the souvenirs and things I don’t need go in the backpack and my everyday stuff is stashed in the suitcase. It’s much easier to pack.

So, it’s now the second day in Hong Kong and we’ve wandered ourselves silly for a few hours. The first stop was “Shanghai Tang”, a modern twist on traditional Chinese style. We both came away with some nice souvenirs. I was tempted by the dresses, but knew none of them would fit anyway. (More on this topic later.)

Next up, an alley lined with photography stores. Geoff had been looking for a replacement lens for his camera for awhile but with no luck any time in the past few weeks. After some false positives, he still didn’t find the one he was hoping for. So we moved on. Our intended next-up was a Dim Sum restaurant the Lonely Planet had raved about. We found it with minimal fuss, but as it turns out, they only serve Dim Sum after 2pm.

So, up the street and down some other roads we went until we found a little sidewalk bar/cafe where we ordered some interestingly named (“Black Sheep Brewing Company”) ales and a plate of potato skins to tide us over until Dim Sum. Of course, now I feel so full I can’t imagine scarfing down dumplings and buns, potstickers and spare ribs. Maybe in a few hours?

And finally, the Linva Tailor in Soho. Linva is recommended in the Lonely Planet as a place to have a cheong sam (traditional Chinese dress) custom made. So, I picked out fabric, and got measured, put down a hefty deposit and agreed to come back tomorrow at 5pm for a fitting. I guess tomorrow won’t be beach day after all.

I’m just hoping it turns out well and is flattering. I’m not feeling all that positive about my body image right now, so with any luck, this will be a good decision and not a waste of time and money. I have something pretty appropriate picked out for the occasion, so here’s hoping!
