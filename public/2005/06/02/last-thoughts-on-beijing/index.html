Last thoughts on Beijing

Filed under: China,Uncategorized — Amy @ 12:27 am

OK, I know this is out of order. But I have an hour to kill and some photos to post so I can clear off my SIM card and take more!

We did end up going to the Forbidden City for a day. The day started off lovely but soon degraded into a thunderstorm. On the way in, we found this cute little park. Oddly quiet and peaceful considering it’s location in the center of Tourist Beijing.

Of course, the park isn’t the reason to go there, the Forbidden City is. Long kept only as a haven for the Emperor and his favorites, the Forbidden City is now open to anyone who wants to pay for admission with a few yuan instead of one’s life.

Probably in preparation for the 2008 Olympics, over 50% of the buildings were under scaffolding for renovation. But, we still got to see a fair bit and appreciate the vastness of the compound.

Proof I need a haircut:

Before we got too far, it started pouring.

And pouring.

By the time we reached the hotel (couldn’t find a cab), we were soaked to the bone.

Maybe I should have bought one of those umbrellas?

I don’t know if I’ve mentioned, but dripping is not really appropriate in the Pennisula Palace Hotel.

Not when they serve dessert that looks like this, anyway.

Or have bathrooms that look like this.

Who says you have to rough it all the time????
